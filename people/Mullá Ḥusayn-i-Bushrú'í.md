# [[Mullá Ḥusayn-i-Bushrú'í]]
*The First Believer and Bábu'l-Báb (Gate of the Gate)*

## Basic Information
- **Full Name/Title**: Mullá Ḥusayn-i-Bushrú'í
- **Birth**: c. 1813 in [[Bushrúyh]], [[Khurásán]]
- **Death**: February 2, 1849 at [[Fort Shaykh Ṭabarsí]]
- **Occupation/Role**: 
  - Islamic scholar and cleric
  - First believer in the Báb
  - Bábu'l-Báb (Gate of the Gate)
  - Military leader of Bábí forces
- **Significance**: 
  - The first person to recognize and accept the Báb
  - Led the heroic defense at [[Fort Shaykh Ṭabarsí]]
  - Exemplar of courage and spiritual devotion

### Early Life and Education

- **Teacher**: Student of [[Siyyid Kázim-i-Rashtí]]
- **Spiritual Quest**: Searched for the Promised Qá'im after his teacher's death

### Recognition of the Báb
- **Date**: May 23, 1844 (5th of Jamádíyu'l-Avval, 1260 A.H.)
- **Location**: [[Shíráz]], house of the Báb
- **Event**: [[Declaration of the Báb]]
- **Significance**: First person to recognize and accept the Báb

### Mission and Travels
- **First Journey**: Traveled to spread the message of the Báb
- **Destinations**: [[Iṣfahán]], [[Káshán]], [[Ṭihrán]], [[Khurásán]]
- **Purpose**: Proclamation of the new Revelation
- **Companions**: Often accompanied by [[Quddús]] and other believers

### Fort Shaykh Ṭabarsí
- **Leadership**: Commander of Bábí forces during the siege
- **Duration**: October 1848 - May 1849
- **Organization**: Instituted martial order and defensive strategies
- **Lieutenant**: Appointed his nephew [[Muḥammad-Báqir]] as aide
- **Martyrdom**: Killed in battle on February 2, 1849
